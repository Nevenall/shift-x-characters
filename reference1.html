Terms

Assets are durable details introduced into the narrative. They have their own ratings and last for the rest of the scene.

Advantages are temporary +2 bonuses with a flavor tag. They last until after your next action. They can be passed to allies.

A call on is allowed for some traits, such as distinctions. When your trait applies to the narrative circumstances you may spend a determination and call on that trait to make a second roll and keep the better result. Alternatively, if you've succeeded on a roll, you may gain a determination if you roll again and keep the worst result.

To concede from a conflict, spend a determination and describe how you are removed from the scene.

A consequence is a negative distinction that represents a wound you have taken.

Characters can be compelled based on their traits, especially distinctions and limits, or the narrative circumstances. Compels can be mechanical, such as shutting down a power; or they can suggest that the character make a bad choice, or suffer bad luck. If you accept a compel you suffer the narrative suggestion and gain a determination, if your refuse the compel you must spend a determination to avoid misfortune.

Determination is our name for Fate Points. It's a metacurrency used mostly for bonuses to rolls and gained with narrative misfortune.

Distinctions are phrases that describe fundamental things about a character. Distinctions can be called on and bring characters vexing misfortune.

A major cost means the narrative situation gets significantly worse or more complicated. Major costs include introducing new problems, bringing in new foes, putting the players on an imminent deadline, inflicting a mild or moderate consequence, and giving the enemy a new advantage or asset.

A minor cost is a story detail about difficulty or complication but isn't a hindrances itself: a few points of stress, an advantage, or doom to the enemy.

Opposition is the rating that opposes your actions. Based on what you are attempting, the narrative circumstances, and villains who oppose you.

SWS stands for Success with Style. It's like a critical hit and indicated that you've achieved something beyond your intent.

Support Extra help from other heroes or related powers.

Taken out occurs when you take damage you cannot absorb. You are removed from the scene in a manner of your foe's choosing.

Trait is a generic word for a mechanical element a character might possess.

Vexing Misfortune occurs when something unavoidable and terrible occurs to the heroes. In return they get determination.
